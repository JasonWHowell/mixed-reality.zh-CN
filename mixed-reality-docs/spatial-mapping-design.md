## <a name="what-influences-spatial-mapping-quality"></a><span data-ttu-id="66491-121">哪些因素会影响空间映射质量？</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-121">What influences spatial mapping quality?</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-122">为了提供最佳的用户体验, 请务必了解影响 HoloLens 收集的空间映射数据质量的因素。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-122">In order to provide the best user experience, it is important to understand the factors that affect the quality of spatial mapping data gathered by HoloLens.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-123">空间映射数据中的错误分为以下三个类别之一:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-123">Errors in spatial mapping data fall into one of three categories:</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="66491-124">**洞**:空间映射数据中缺少实际的表面。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-124">**Holes**: Real-world surfaces are missing from the spatial mapping data.</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="66491-125">**Hallucinations**:图面存在于现实世界中不存在的空间映射数据中。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-125">**Hallucinations**: Surfaces exist in the spatial mapping data that do not exist in the real world.</span></span>
* <span data-ttu-id="66491-126">**偏向**:空间映射数据中的图面与实际表面生成对齐或拉出。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-126">**Bias**: Surfaces in the spatial mapping data are imperfectly aligned with real-world surfaces, either pushed in or pulled out.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-127">几个因素可能会影响这些错误的频率和严重性:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-127">Several factors can affect the frequency and severity of these errors:</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-128">**用户运动**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-128">**User motion**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-129">用户在其环境中的移动方式决定了环境的扫描情况, 因此用户可能需要指导才能实现良好的扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-129">How the user moves through their environment determines how well the environment will be scanned, so the user may require guidance in order to achieve a good scan.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-130">用于扫描的相机在70度的圆锥范围内提供数据, 从最小0.8 米到最大3.1 米距离相机。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-130">The camera used for scanning provides data within a 70-degree cone, from a minimum of 0.8 meters to a maximum of 3.1 meters distance from the camera.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-131">实际的图面将仅在此视图字段内扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-131">Real-world surfaces will only be scanned within this field of view.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-132">请注意, 这些值在将来的版本中可能会更改。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-132">Note that these values are subject to change in future versions.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-133">如果用户永远不会获得对象的3.1 米以内, 则不会对其进行扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-133">If the user never gets within 3.1 meters of an object then it will not be scanned.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-134">如果用户仅查看小于0.8 米的距离的对象, 则不会对其进行扫描 (这将避免扫描用户的手)。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-134">If the user only views an object from a distance of less than 0.8 meters then it will not be scanned (this avoids scanning the user's hands).</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-135">如果用户永不向上查看 (相当正常), 则可能不会扫描天花板。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-135">If the user never looks upward (which is fairly normal) then the ceiling will likely not be scanned.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-136">如果用户不会在家具或墙壁后面显示, 则不会扫描封闭像素的对象。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-136">If a user never looks behind furniture or a wall then the objects occluded by them will not be scanned.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-137">在表面上查看时, 表面的扫描速度会更高, 而不是在浅时扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-137">Surfaces tend to be scanned at a higher quality when they are viewed head-on rather than at a shallow angle.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-138">如果 HoloLens 的打印头跟踪系统很快就会失败 (这可能是由于快速的用户动作、光照不佳、featureless 墙壁或相机被覆盖而导致的), 这可能会导致空间映射数据出错。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-138">If the head-tracking system of the HoloLens fails momentarily (which may happen due to rapid user motion, poor lighting, featureless walls or the cameras becoming covered), this can introduce errors in the spatial mapping data.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-139">当用户继续移动和扫描其环境时, 任何此类错误都会得到更正。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-139">Any such errors will be corrected over time as the user continues to move around and scan their environment.</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-140">**Surface 材料**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-140">**Surface materials**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-141">实际图面上发现的材料差别很大。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-141">The materials found on real-world surfaces vary greatly.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-142">这会影响空间映射数据的质量, 因为它们会影响红外光源的反射方式。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-142">These impact the quality of spatial mapping data because they affect how infrared light is reflected.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-143">暗表面可能无法扫描, 直到它们接近相机, 因为它们的光线更小。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-143">Dark surfaces may not scan until they come closer to the camera, because they reflect less light.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-144">某些表面可能会很暗, 它们反映出的光线太少, 无法从任何距离进行扫描, 因此, 它们会在表面位置和曲面后面的位置引入孔错误。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-144">Some surfaces may be so dark that they reflect too little light to be scanned from any distance, so they will introduce hole errors at the location of the surface and sometimes also behind the surface.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-145">特别有光泽的表面只能在观看时扫描, 而不能在从浅角度观看时扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-145">Particularly shiny surfaces may only scan when viewed head-on, and not when viewed from a shallow angle.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-146">镜像, 因为它们创建了实空格和曲面的 illusory 反射, 因此可能导致孔洞错误和 hallucination 错误。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-146">Mirrors, because they create illusory reflections of real spaces and surfaces, can cause both hole errors and hallucination errors.</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-147">**场景运动**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-147">**Scene motion**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-148">空间映射可以对环境中的更改 (例如移动人员或打开和结束门) 进行快速调整。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-148">Spatial mapping adapts rapidly to changes in the environment, such as moving people or opening and closing doors.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-149">但是, 只有当区域对用于扫描的照相机清晰可见时, 空间映射才能适应区域中的更改。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-149">However, spatial mapping can only adapt to changes in an area when that area is clearly visible to the camera that is used for scanning.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-150">因此, 这种情况可能会使这种调整滞后于现实, 这可能会导致洞或 hallucination 错误。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-150">Because of this, it is possible for this adaptation to lag behind reality, which can cause hole or hallucination errors.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-151">例如, 用户扫描朋友, 然后在朋友离开房间时翻过来。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-151">As an example, a user scans a friend and then turns around while the friend leaves the room.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-152">朋友的 "ghost" 表示形式 (hallucination 错误) 将保留在空间映射数据中, 直到用户返回并重新扫描朋友所在的空间。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-152">A 'ghost' representation of the friend (a hallucination error) will persist in the spatial mapping data, until the user turns back around and re-scans the space where the friend was standing.</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-153">**光照干扰**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-153">**Lighting interference**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-154">场景中的环境红外线灯可能会干扰扫描, 例如, 通过窗口进入强大的阳光。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-154">Ambient infrared light in the scene may interfere with scanning, for example strong sunlight coming through a window.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-155">特别有光泽的图面可能会干扰附近表面的扫描, 而光线会导致偏移错误。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-155">Particularly shiny surfaces may interfere with the scanning of nearby surfaces, the light bouncing off them causing bias errors.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-156">视频面直接将光反射回相机可能会干扰附近的 hallucinations, 也可能会导致浮动的中端, 或延迟调整到场景运动。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-156">Shiny surfaces reflecting light directly back into the camera may interfere with nearby space, either by causing floating mid-air hallucinations or by delaying adaptation to scene motion.</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-157">同一房间中的两个 HoloLens 设备不应相互干扰, 但有超过5个 HoloLens 设备可能会造成干扰。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-157">Two HoloLens devices in the same room should not interfere with one another, but the presence of more than five HoloLens devices may cause interference.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-158">对于其中一些错误, 可能会避免或更正此问题。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-158">It may be possible to avoid or correct for some of these errors.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-159">但是, 你应设计应用程序, 以便即使在空间映射数据中存在错误时, 用户也能够实现其目标。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-159">However, you should design your application so that the user is able to achieve their goals even in the presence of errors in the spatial mapping data.</span></span>

## <a name="the-environment-scanning-experience"></a><span data-ttu-id="66491-160">环境扫描体验</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-160">The environment scanning experience</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-161">HoloLens 在用户查看其环境中的表面时了解到它们。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-161">HoloLens learns about the surfaces in its environment as the user looks at them.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-162">随着时间的推移, HoloLens 会生成一个扫描环境中所有部件的扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-162">Over time, the HoloLens builds up a scan of all parts of the environment that have been observed.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-163">它还会在观察到环境中的更改时更新扫描。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-163">It also updates the scan as changes in the environment are observed.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-164">此扫描将在应用启动时自动保留。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-164">This scan will automatically persist between app launches.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-165">使用空间映射的每个应用程序都应考虑提供 "扫描体验";应用程序引导用户扫描应用程序正常运行所必需的表面所使用的过程。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-165">Each application that uses spatial mapping should consider providing a 'scanning experience'; the process through which the application guides the user to scan surfaces that are necessary for the application to function correctly.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-167">*扫描示例*</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-167">*Example of scanning*</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-168">此扫描体验的性质可能因每个应用程序的需求而有很大差别, 但两个主要原则应指导其设计。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-168">The nature of this scanning experience can vary greatly depending upon each application's needs, but two main principles should guide its design.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-169">首先,**清除与用户的通信是主要关注**点。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-169">Firstly, **clear communication with the user is the primary concern**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-170">用户应始终知道是否满足应用程序的要求。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-170">The user should always be aware of whether the application's requirements are being met.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-171">如果不满足这些要求, 则应该立即清楚地说明这种情况的原因, 并且应迅速采取适当的措施。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-171">When they are not being met, it should be immediately clear to the user why this is so and they should be quickly led to take the appropriate action.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-172">其次,**应用程序应尝试在效率和可靠性之间取得平衡**。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-172">Secondly, **applications should attempt to strike a balance between efficiency and reliability**.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-173">如果可以**可靠地**执行此操作, 则应用程序应自动分析空间映射数据以节省用户时间。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-173">When it is possible to do so **reliably**, applications should automatically analyze spatial mapping data to save the user time.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-174">如果无法可靠地执行此操作, 则应用程序应改为允许用户使用其所需的其他信息快速提供应用程序。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-174">When it is not possible to do so reliably, applications should instead enable the user to quickly provide the application with the additional information it requires.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-175">若要帮助设计正确的扫描体验, 请考虑以下哪些可能适用于你的应用程序:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-175">To help design the right scanning experience, consider which of the following possibilities are applicable to your application:</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-176">**无扫描体验**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-176">**No scanning experience**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-177">如果没有任何引导式扫描体验, 应用程序可以正常工作;它将了解在自然用户移动过程中观察到的图面。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-177">An application may function perfectly without any guided scanning experience; it will learn about surfaces that are observed in the course of natural user movement.</span></span>

## <a name="mesh-processing"></a><span data-ttu-id="66491-223">网格处理</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-223">Mesh processing</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-224">它可能有助于检测图面中的常见错误类型, 并根据需要对空间映射数据进行筛选、删除或修改。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-224">It may help to detect common types of errors in surfaces and to filter, remove or modify the spatial mapping data as appropriate.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-225">请记住, 空间映射数据应尽可能忠实到实际的表面, 因此, 任何正在进行的处理都将进一步从 "真实" 改变你的曲面。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-225">Bear in mind that spatial mapping data is intended to be as faithful as possible to real-world surfaces, so any processing you apply risks shifting your surfaces further from the 'truth'.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="66491-226">下面是一些可能有用的不同类型网格处理的示例:</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-226">Here are some examples of different types of mesh processing that you may find useful:</span></span>

* <span data-ttu-id="66491-227">**孔洞填充**</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-227">**Hole filling**</span></span>
   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-228">如果导致暗材料的小对象无法扫描, 它将在周围表面上留下一个孔。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-228">If a small object made of a dark material fails to scan, it will leave a hole in the surrounding surface.</span></span>

   * <span data-ttu-id="66491-244">可以从 surface 网格筛选此类 hallucinations。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-244">It is possible to filter such hallucinations from the surface mesh.</span></span> <span data-ttu-id="66491-245">但是, 与孔洞一样, 你需要调整算法, 使真实的小对象 (如灯具和门控点) 不会被删除。</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="66491-245">However, as with holes, you will need to tune your algorithm so that real small objects such as lamp-stands and door handles do not get removed.</span></span>
